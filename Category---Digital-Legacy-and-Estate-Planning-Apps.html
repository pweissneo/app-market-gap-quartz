The Six Core Gaps

1. Digital Assets Are Largely Undocumented

The failure:

  • Average person has 100+ online accounts
  • Photos stored in iCloud, Google Photos, Facebook — family can’t access
  • Crypto wallets: keys lost = assets lost forever
  • Subscriptions still charging deceased’s card for months
  • No “digital life inventory” that documents everything

What should exist:

  • “Digital estate inventory” — document all accounts, passwords, assets with inheritance instructions
  • Account discovery: “Based on your email, here’s 100+ accounts we found — which do you want to plan for?”
  • Structured inheritance instructions per account type

2. Death Notification Administration

The failure:

  • When someone dies, 50-100 accounts need to be identified and notified
  • Social media, subscriptions, financial accounts, loyalty programs
  • No “death notification checklist” that generates based on known accounts
  • Family discovers accounts through guesswork and email searching

What should exist:

  • “Death notification service” — pre-planned notification list that activates on death
  • Generate checklist: “Here are all accounts to notify and how”
  • Social media account memorialization requests, subscription cancellations

3. Crypto and Digital Assets Specifically

The failure:

  • $200B+ in lost crypto (keys lost, forgotten wallets)
  • Hardware wallets: if family doesn’t know it exists, it’s lost forever
  • No proper inheritance tools for crypto
  • “I have crypto but my family will never find it if I die”

What should exist:

  • “Crypto inheritance planner” — proper setup for crypto estate transfer
  • Multi-signature inheritance schemes
  • Instructions that survive: “Here’s how my family finds and inherits my crypto”
  • Integration with major exchanges for death notifications

4. Incapacity Planning — Digital Access During Coma

The failure:

  • Incapacity planning: who can access your accounts if you’re in a coma?
  • Apple Legacy Contact, Google Inactive Account Manager — first steps only
  • Medical records, insurance portals, care coordination — no digital incapacity plan
  • Healthcare proxy needs digital access but no guidance on what or how

What should exist:

  • “Incapacity planning digital guide” — what your healthcare proxy needs access to and why
  • Medical portal access, insurance accounts, care coordination tools
  • Pre-authorized access that activates on incapacity certification

5. Family History and Memory Preservation

The failure:

  • Photos scattered across phones, cloud services, social media
  • No one has all the photos — they exist in fragments
  • “Tell me about Mom’s life” — photos are disorganized, stories are lost
  • Family history in email inboxes, Facebook messages, WhatsApp groups

What should exist:

  • “Family memory aggregator” — gather photos and stories about family members
  • Scan old photos, connect to social media, organize by person and time
  • “This is your grandmother’s life in photos and stories”
  • Collaborative family memory building

6. Pet Care in Estate Planning

The failure:

  • Who takes the pets if you die? Most estate plans don’t specify
  • Pets often re-homed or surrendered because no plan existed
  • Financial provision for pet care — usually absent
  • No: “Your estate plan should include a pet care directive”

What should exist:

  • “Pet care estate directive” — who takes the pets, who pays for care
  • Financial provision tracker: “Set aside $X for pet care in your estate”
  • Care instructions for each pet: feeding, medical, routine
